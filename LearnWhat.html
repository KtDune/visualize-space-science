Animals

Research in animal biology under the program explores how animals adapt to spaceflight and gravity alterations, using model organisms like rodents and invertebrates. Questions asked include the effects of spaceflight over time, its permanence, and conditions needed for animal health post-return from space.

Cells

Cell and molecular biology studies at NASA delve into how spaceflight conditions impact living systems at cellular and molecular levels, focusing on genetic and molecular mechanisms altered by gravitational changes and space environment. Factors like DNA function, cellular structure, and cell-to-cell communication are assessed.

Microbiology

Microbiology research considers the effects of spaceflight on microbial populations, including health risks to astronauts and damage to spacecraft equipment. The role of microbes in Earth's ecosystems is also explored, emphasizing the importance of microbial equilibrium for successful space missions.

Plants

Plant biology research examines how microgravity affects plant growth at the molecular level, aiming to support astronauts with fresh food during long missions. Investigations on the ISS have successfully grown lettuce and cabbage, with future experiments planning to introduce Mizuna and tomatoes.
